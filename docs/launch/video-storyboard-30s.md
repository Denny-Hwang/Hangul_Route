> social, horizontal 16:9 for PH. Cut both from the same source clip.

## Voiceover script (30 seconds, English)

> *(0s)* Your child knows ABC.
> *(4s)* But what about 한글 — Hangul — the Korean alphabet their grandma uses?
> *(10s)* Hangul Route is a 5-minute quest a day.
> *(14s)* A tiger named Hoya teaches one letter, plays one mini-game, gives one heritage card.
> *(20s)* Twenty-four cards in Stage 1. One Korea, one child at a time.
> *(27s)* Hangul Route. Korean for kids who don't speak it — yet.

If self-recorded VO: pace 165–175 WPM, warm tone, smile while reading.

---

## Music

- **License**: use royalty-free Korean-influenced soft kalimba +
  gayageum (Epidemic Sound: "Hanji Morning" or similar).
- **Tempo**: 70 BPM throughout, no swells.
- **Mix**: voice at -6 dB, music at -22 dB, SFX at -14 dB.

---

## Caption / accessibility

- Burned-in English captions for every spoken word (PH plays muted by
  default).
- A separate `.srt` file for accessibility upload.
- Every Korean character on screen gets a romanization line under it.

## Production checklist

- [ ] Phone in shot is iPhone 13/14/15 (PH crowd skews iPhone)
- [ ] Child's hand: aged 5–8, no nail polish, no jewelry
- [ ] No real names/identifiable kids' faces (face-blur or hand-only)
- [ ] All Korean text on screen has romanization
- [ ] No red on the wrong-answer moment (we never show one anyway —
      shot #5 shows only the correct tap)
- [ ] Quiet room — bedroom carpet, not a kitchen
- [ ] Three takes minimum, use take 2 (always the natural one)
