# Parent Interview Guide — 30 minutes, 5 parents

> **Goal**: in the same D-7 / D-6 window as the hallway tests, run 5
> short interviews with the *buyers* — parents in P4 (heritage Korean)
> and P5 (K-culture curious). The kids tell us if the app *works*.
> Parents tell us if they'd *pay*.

---

## Who to recruit

| # | Persona | Where to find them |
|---|---|---|
| P-01 | Korean-American mom, kid age 6 | KAYA, Korean church mom groups |
| P-02 | Mixed-heritage dad, kid age 8 | Reddit r/KoreanAmerican, friend-of-friend |
| P-03 | Non-Korean parent, kid age 9, "wants K-culture exposure" | Facebook K-pop fan parent groups |
| P-04 | Bilingual Korean parent already teaching, kid age 7 | Sejong Hakdang parents |
| P-05 | Korean grandparent buying for grandchild | local Korean senior center |

If you can't get 5, get 3. **Two is too few** (no triangulation).

---

## Logistics

- 30 minutes per interview.
- Zoom is fine; in-person is better.
- Record (with consent) — audio only.
- $40 thank-you gift card per parent. Budget for it.
- Send the demo video (`docs/launch/video-storyboard-30s.md` final cut)
  48 hours before the call. They'll arrive with reactions.

---

## The script

Use the same five sections in the same order across all 5 interviews.
**Do not improvise.** Repeatability is the whole point.

### Section 1 — Their world (5 minutes)

> "Tell me about your kid's relationship with Korean. Not 'do they
> speak it' — but where Korean shows up in their life."

Listen for:
- Korean *people* (grandparent, classmate, parent)
- Korean *media* (K-pop, K-drama, YouTube)
- Korean *food* (the most common entry point)
- A specific moment when the kid asked about Korean and the parent
  couldn't answer

That last moment is the buying trigger. **Write it down verbatim.**

### Section 2 — Their current solutions (5 minutes)

> "What have you tried before to teach them Korean — apps, books,
> classes, in-laws, anything?"

Listen for:
- What they bought and abandoned, and why
- The price ceiling they hit ("$15/month is too much for an app")
- The friction that killed previous attempts (drive distance,
  homework load, kid resistance)
- Apps named by memory — that's the competitive set in their head.
  **Not what the App Store says.**

### Section 3 — Reaction to Hangul Route (10 minutes)

> "I'd love to walk you through what I built and get your honest
> reaction."

Share the 30-second video, then the PH gallery deck. Don't pitch
the price yet.

Listen for:
- The 3 seconds where their eyes light up
- The 3 seconds where they lose focus
- Words they reach for unprompted ("cute," "soft," "premium," "kid-
  friendly," "babyish," "for school," "for play")
- The first question they ask. The first question is always the real
  question — even if you go on to answer twenty.

### Section 4 — Price + commitment (5 minutes)

> "If you wanted this in your house, would you pay for it? How?"

Pose three pricing models and watch their face:
1. Free with 12 cards locked, $4.99 per 12-card pack
2. $29 lifetime
3. $4.99/month subscription

The model they spend more than 5 seconds on is the model they'd
actually buy. The one they reject in under 2 seconds tells you about
the *category* of price they refuse.

Follow-up: "If a friend gave you a free year of this, would you
recommend it to one specific person?" — get the name. That's the
referral mechanic for the launch.

### Section 5 — The killer question (3 minutes)

> "On a scale of 1–10, how likely are you to use this with your kid
> next month? And — be honest — what would have to be true for you to
> say 10?"

Listen for the gap. The 10-condition is the next 6 weeks of work.
Top three patterns I expect to hear:
- "If my mother-in-law approves." → cultural sign-off matters
- "If it works on the iPad." → device support
- "If it has Stage 2 already." → content depth

If the median answer is < 6, the PH launch is premature. Push the
date.

### Section 6 — Wrap (2 minutes)

> "Anything I didn't ask that I should have?"

Always ask this. Half the best insights live here.

> "Can I email you again before launch? And — would you be willing
> to leave a 1-sentence comment on Product Hunt if I send you a link
> the morning of launch?"

Yes/no, no pressure. Track in the squad spreadsheet from
`reddit-seeding.md`.

## Triage across the 5 interviews

After all 5 are done, look for:

1. **3+ interviews share the same "buying trigger" pattern** → that
   becomes the PH page lede + the maker comment v1.
2. **3+ interviews give the same 10-condition** → that's the next 6
   weeks of work, in priority order.
3. **The median price model** → that's what ships at PH.
4. **The 5 referral names they gave you** → that's your first 25
   personally-invited beta users.

Output: one page of decisions, committed to
`docs/launch/parent-interview-findings__YYYY-MM-DD.md`.

## Pitfalls to avoid

- ❌ **Defending the app.** When a parent says "this feels babyish,"
  the answer is "tell me more" — not "well, actually…"
- ❌ **Asking what they want.** Parents don't know what they want.
  Ask what they *did* (recent purchases, recent abandonments).
- ❌ **Selling at the end.** The interview is the data — don't ruin
  it with a closing pitch. The TestFlight link goes in the thank-you
  email the next day, not the call.
- ❌ **Recruiting only Korean parents.** P5 (curious non-Korean) is a
  third of the launch audience. Skipping them means you ship for the
  wrong median user.
- ❌ **Talking more than 30% of the time.** If your audio file shows
  you talking more than the parent, the interview failed.
